Québec's video game industry didn't happen by accident. It was built over four decades by thousands of developers, artists, designers, and entrepreneurs who chose to bet on games as a legitimate cultural and economic force.

The story often begins in 1997, when Ubisoft opened its Montréal studio — a landmark moment that proved Québec could compete at the highest level of global game development. That bet paid off spectacularly: Ubisoft Montréal became one of the largest game studios in the world, home to some of the industry's most iconic franchises.

But the real story is broader. In 2003, La Guilde du jeu vidéo du Québec was founded to support and unite the growing ecosystem — from giants like Eidos-Montréal and WB Games Montréal to indie powerhouses like Sabotage Studio, Thunder Lotus, and Red Barrels. Today, the province is home to over 200 studios, generating more than 1.4 billion dollars in annual revenue and employing over 14,000 people.

Games from Québec is a testament to that story — and a commitment to keeping it growing.
